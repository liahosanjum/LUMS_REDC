PROGRAMME OVERVIEW

 

 

 

The Management Development Programme (MDP) is a flagship executive education programme, developed for functional managers, business unit heads and other professionals who are planning successful career transitions from functional to general management levels. The programme allows the participants to expand their knowledge base across all functional areas and develop a holistic management perspective. By integrating key functional disciplines, the participants develop the capability to envision, influence and control the strategic direction of the firm. Residing together at LUMS, the participants immerse themselves in a two week-long learning experience that has been engineered to be rigorous, real in context and transformational.

The Management Development Programme at LUMS enables the participants to integrate key functional areas in an organisation and drive a result-focused business strategy. The objective of MDP is to create successful general managers who can compete by leveraging their own and their organisations’ inherent strengths in an integrated manner.

PROGRAMME BENEFITS

The Management Development Programme will help participants;
• Acquire conceptual skills to understand business functions and their inter relationships
• Develop the ability to rethink business functions and lead change at different levels of an organisation
• Understand how industry structures work and evaluate how to create value and profit for their operating unit in a competitive environment
• Develop key value drivers for their business performance to achieve long-term growth and sustenance
• Learn international best practices as to how organisations respond to recession and cyclicality
• Understand and harness your leadership capability to lead the organisation effectively
• Gain from networking opportunities with like-minded people from diverse backgrounds/industry experiences

PROGRAMME CURRICULUM

 
Module 1 has been designed to cover broad functional areas in order to enhance the general management skills and expose participants to other areas of management in which they may have limited knowledge and/or activities.

Module 2 focuses on the abilities of the participants to view the organisation from a strategic perspective and in the process sharpen their leadership, interpersonal and negotiation skills. This would increase their impact as leaders and change agents.

WHO SHOULD ATTEND

The programme is designed for high potential senior executives who have either taken-on, or are preparing to transit from functional to broader general management positions. Business unit heads, experienced functional managers, chief operating officers, regional directors may be suitable candidates for this programme, a typical participant profile includes;

• Country Managers • Directors • Divisional Heads • Senior Managers
• Controllers • Vice Presidents • CFOs
